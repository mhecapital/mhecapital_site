Nasdaq Futures – 5-Tick Scalping Algorithm

This model is a fast-execution, micro-impulse scalping strategy designed specifically for Nasdaq futures. It combines a short stop-loss structure (5 ticks) with a volatility-driven confirmation indicator to catch rapid directional bursts during high-volume periods.

1. Overview

The strategy uses a 5-tick stop, 7.5-tick take profit (RR 1.5), and an automatic break-even trigger. It works best during the US cash open, when volatility and liquidity conditions favor momentum bursts.

  • Timeframes: 15s to 1m
  • Stop-loss: 5 ticks
  • Take profit: 7.5 ticks (RR = 1.5)
  • Break-even: +3 ticks
  • Indicator: SuperTrend (3,7) or ATR-based volatility band
  • Best Session: 9:30–10:15 ET

2. Strategy Logic

Long Setup

  • SuperTrend flips from red → green
  • Price retests band or micro pullback
  • Bullish confirmation candle
  • Execute:
    • Market buy
    • SL = 5 ticks
    • TP = 7.5 ticks
    • BE at +3 ticks

Short Setup

  • SuperTrend flips from green → red
  • Retest + bearish confirmation
  • Market sell with same risk rules

3. Optimal Conditions

  • High volatility (US Open)
  • 1-tick spread
  • Clear orderflow pressure
  • No news in next 5 minutes

4. Trade Management

The method relies on strict capital protection. The break-even mechanism eliminates many small losses, while the RR 1.5 target captures strong micro-pulses.

5. Example Workflow

  1. Wait for SuperTrend flip
  2. Take the first pullback
  3. Break-even after +3 ticks
  4. Let TP hit or exit early on volume collapse
